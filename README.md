# Employee-attrition-Prediction

## 📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations because replacing employees requires both time and cost. The goal of this project is to analyze employee data, identify the key factors that influence attrition, and build machine learning models to predict whether an employee is likely to leave the company.

This project was completed as part of my Week 2 Data Science Internship assignment.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition data.
- Perform data cleaning and preprocessing.
- Explore the dataset using visualizations.
- Build machine learning models for attrition prediction.
- Compare different models and evaluate their performance.
- Provide HR insights and business recommendations based on the analysis.

---

## 📂 Dataset

**Dataset Name:** IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance

The dataset contains information such as:

- Age
- Department
- Job Role
- Monthly Income
- Years at Company
- Overtime
- Work-Life Balance
- Education
- Job Satisfaction
- Attrition (Target Variable)

## 📈 Key Findings

- Employees working overtime have a higher chance of leaving.
- Lower monthly income is associated with higher attrition.
- Employees with fewer years at the company are more likely to resign.
- Sales Representatives and Laboratory Technicians show relatively higher attrition.
- Employee attrition depends on multiple factors rather than salary alone.

---

## 💡 HR Recommendations

- Improve employee engagement for high-risk departments.
- Review compensation for employees with lower salaries.
- Introduce mentoring programs for newly hired employees.
- Reduce excessive overtime whenever possible.
- Conduct regular employee feedback sessions.
